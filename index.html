Law - 3

(Never Revel Your Intesions)

1. Use decoyed object of desire and red-hirings to throw people of scent.

2. Use smoke screens to disguise your actions.

3. False sincerity is one powerful tool that will send you reveals on a wild goose chase.

Law - 4

(Always Say Lesser Than Necessary)

1. Saying less will keep you from saying something foolish or even dangerous.

2. Once the words are out, you cannot take them back.

3. Keeping silent make people reveal more about themselves.This is the information you may be able to use against them later on.

Law - 5

(So Much Depend On Reputation, Guard It With Your Life)

1. Show doubt and spread rumours about your rival. Even if they vehemently deny it, people will still be wondering why they are so defensive.

2. Use humor or gentle mockery at your rival's expense.

3. A solid reputation increases your presence and exaggeratesyour strength without your having to spend much energy.

4. Never appear desperate in your self-defense against the slander of others.

5. Be careful not to go too far attacking another's reputation, it draws more attention to your vegefulness than to the person you are slanding. Use subtler tactic like satire & ridicule.

Law - 6

(Court Attention At All Cost)

1. Surround your name with the sensational and the scandalous.

2. Create an air of mystery.

3. It is better to be attacked & slandered then ignored.

4. Make yourself appear larger than life.

5. Any sort of notoriety will bring you power.

Law - 7

(Get Others To Do The Work For You, But Always Take The Credit)

1. Save time & energy by hiring others to do the work.

2. Your helpers will be forgotten & you'll be remembered.

3. Borrow from history. Use the part & profit from others' experiences.

4. You can only exploit other's talents if your position is unshakable.

Law - 8

(make other people come to you, Use a bit of necessary)

1. For negotiations and meetings, it is wise to lure others into your territory. Or a territory of your choice.

2. Once someone suspects you are manipulating him, it'll be harder to control him. Making him come to you gives the illusion that he is in control.

3. Most often the effective action is to stay back, keep calm, and let others be frustrated by the traps you set for them.

Law - 9

(Win through your actions, never through your arguments)

1. Demonstrate, do not explicate.

2. Arguing will only offend your superior.

3. Learn to demonstrate the correctness of your ideas indirectly.

4. Choose your battles carefully.

Law - 10

(Infection: avoid the unhappy and the unlucky)

1. In the game of power, the people you associate with are critical.

2. An inferior can be recognized by the misfortune they draw on themselves, their turbulent past, a long line of broken relationships, unstable careers, the varying intensity of their emotion, and the force of their character.

3. Gravitate towards prosperous, cheerful, & gregorius people.

4. Never associate with those who share your defects.

Law - 11

(learn to keep people dependent on you)

1. Be the only one who can do what you do. Make the fate of those who hire you so entwined with yours that they cannot possibly get rid of it.

2. If you're ambitious, it is wiser to seek out weak masters with whom you can create a relationship of dependency.

3. Possess a talent or creative skill that sets you apart from the crowd.

4. By knowing that people's secrets and holding info they would not want to be made public, you seal your fate with theirs.

Law - 12

(use selective honesty & generosity to disarm your victim)

1. The essence of deception is distraction. An act of kindness, generosity, or honesty will distract & disarm people & turn them into gullible children.

2. Give before you take.

Law - 13

(when asking for help, appeal to people's self-interest, never to their mercy or gratitude)

1. Understand the other person's motivation.

2. See things their way and offer suggestions that'll advance their cause.

3. For others who want to feel superior & do not want to appear selfish, appeal to their need to display their charity in the public eye.

Law - 14

(pose as a friend, work as a spy)

1. Gather information at social events when people's guards are down.

2. Use other people to give you the information you need.

3. Mislead others by giving out false information. Watch them react & base your next action on what you discover.

Law - 15

(crush your enemy totally)

1. Show no mercy. Crush your rivals or else you'll give them time to regroup & plot their revenge.

2. Vanish enemies to plot for the best time to render them harmless.

3. Leave your enemies no options.

4. Sometimes the enemy will destroy themselves.

5. Thoughts or reconciliation will open you up to attack.

Law - 16

(use absence to increase respect & honour)

1. Create value through scarcity. Make yourself less accessible; otherwise, the aura you've created around yourself will wear away.

Law - 17

(Keep others in suspended terror: cultivate an air of unpredictability)

1. A person of power instils fear by deliberately unsetting those around him to keep the initiative on his side.

2. Only the terminally subordinate act in a predictable manner.

Law - 18

(Don't build a fortress to protect yourself - Isolation is very dangerous)

1. A fortress may be impregnable, but everyone knows that you're there & it may easily turn into a prison.

2. Power depends on social interaction & circulation.

3. Isolation is deadly for the creative arts.

4. Mobility & social contract protects you from plotters.

(know who you're dealing with - do not offend the wrong person)

Do not deal with these people

  1. An arrogant & proud man.
  2. The hopelessly insecure man.
  3. Mr.suspicion
  4. The serpent with a long memory.
  5. The plain.

1. Major your opponent, but never rely on instinct. Do some research on concrete facts about that person's character & history.

2. Never trust appearances.

Law - 20

(do not commit to anyone)

DO NOT CUMMIT WHEN you're extremely happy or when you're extremely unhappy. Say "I'll try"

Law - 21

(play a sucker to catch a sucker)

Everyone wants recognition (Shreya)

Law - 22

(use the surrender tactic: transform weakness into power)

1. Do not fight aggression with aggression. Put your opponent off-guard by yielding, & in effect have more control over the situation.

2. Surrender is a way of mocking your enemies.

3. Surrender disguises your real motives & gives you time to plan your next move.

Law - 23

(Concentrate your forces)

1. Single-mindedness of purpose & total concentration on one goal will overwhelm the enemy every time.

2. A single patron appreciates your loyalty & becomes dependent on your services.

3. In the arts being too single-minded can make you an intolerable bore.

Law -25

(Re-create your-self)

1. Control your appearances & emotions. Play sincere, but not necessarily be sincere.

2. Create a memorable character. Do not limit yourself that society assigns to you.

Law - 26

(Keep your hands clean)

1. Conceal your mistakes. Your good name & reputation depends more on what you conceal than on what you revel.

2. Always have a convenient scapegoat.

3. Never do dirty work yourself.

Law - 27

(play on people's need to believe & create a cult-like following)

1. Keep it simple. Keep it vague. Create new words for vague concepts.

2. Emphasize the visual sensual over the intellectual.

3. Borrow the forms of organised religion to structure the group. Create rituals. Use names & ranks & titles. Ask them to make sacrifices & give alms. Act like a guru or a prophet.

4. Disguise your source of income.

5. Set up an us-vs-them dynamic. Keep followers united by identifying outsiders as devious enemies.

6. The tendency to doubt & reason is broken down when we join a group.

Law - 28

(Enter the action with boldness)

1. The bolder the lie, the better.

2. Lions circle the hesitant prey.

3. The boldness strikes fear; fear creates authority.

4. Going halfway digs the deeper grave. Do not negotiate if your opponent will likely take the opportunity to destroy you.

5. Hesitation creates gaps. Boldness obliterates them. Move swiftly & surely.

6. Audacity separates you from the herd.

Law - 29

(Plan all the way to the end)

1. Take into account all possible obstacles & circumstances that may prevent you from achieving your goal, and plan how you'll overcome them.

2. When you see several septs ahead, you'll no longer need to improvise along the way, and risk deviating from your plan.

3. Prepare alternatives and be open to adapt new routes to your goal.

Law - 30

(make your accomplishments seem effortless)

1. What imitates nature by appearing effortless & natural approximates nature's power.

2. Never show your work until it is finished. When people see the effort & time it takes to make it. And if they witness a work-in-progress. The magic of the finished piece is spoiled.

Law - 31

(control the options: get others to play with the cards you deal)

1. Make people your puppets & give them opetions to let them feel that they control.

2. Force them to choose between the lesser of the two evils that serve your purpose.

3. Present options but colour the one you prefer as the best solution.

4. Force the resister into "Choosing" to do what you want by appearing to advocate the opposite.

5. Alter the playing field so that the only option available is the one you offer.

6. The shrinking option forces people to buy in now, or the good won't be available tomorrow.

7. The weakman must be propelled into action through fear & terror.

8. Involving your victim in your scheme with the threat of your exposure later will keep them tied to you. They cannot expose you because they will be found out as well.

9. Use the horns of dilemma: whichever way they choose, there is no escape.
